📊 Elevation Profile — Huibangsa Course (Huibang Ranger Station → Yeonhwabong Peak)

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Huibang Ranger Station→ Huibangpokpo Falls (28m)→ Huibangsa Temple Exploration→ Kkaldakgogae Pass→ Yeonhwabong Peak 1,394m ★→ Sobaeksan Astronomical Observatory (Optional)→ 🅿 Huibang Original Point Descent

Course Features

  • 🌊 Huibangpokpo Falls (28m high) — Gyeongbuk's largest inland waterfall, scenic all four seasons
  • ✅ Thousand-year-old temple exploration course passing through Huibangsa Temple, founded in 643 AD during the Silla Dynasty
  • ✅ Gentle ridge after Kkaldakgogae Pass — 360-degree panoramic view from Yeonhwabong Peak
  • ✅ Sobaeksan National Park Observatory (near Yeonhwabong Peak) visit possible — Operated by Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute
  • Kkaldakgogae Pass section steep incline — caution for beginners (approx. 400m)
  • Huibang Information Center → Yeonhwabong Peak: 3.7km one-way / 7.4km round-trip
Detailed Route by Section
Exit
Depart from Huibang Ranger Station
700m above sea level · Punggi-eup, Yeongju-si, Gyeongbuk

Start from the parking lot in front of Huibang Ranger Station. After checking the course at the ranger station, begin your ascent. Pay the entrance fee for the Huibangsa District of Sobaeksan National Park (1,600 won for adults).

The Huibang Information Center parking lot is small. During peak season weekends, it fills up early. An early morning start is recommended. Use the restroom at the trail entrance before starting your hike.
1
Huibangpokpo Falls → Huibangsa Temple
Approx. 1.0km · 30 min · Elevation ↑100m
Difficulty

From Huibang Information Center, walk approximately 1km along the valley path to reach Huibangpokpo Waterfall (28m high). It is the largest waterfall in inland Gyeongbuk, displaying different appearances throughout the four seasons. Climb the wooden deck stairs next to the waterfall to arrive at Huibangsa Temple (founded in 643, the 12th year of Queen Seondeok of Silla).

Huibangpokpo Falls is famous for its spectacular frozen icefall in winter. In summer, its refreshing spray makes it a popular spot to escape the heat.
2
Huibangsa Temple → Kkaldakgogae Pass
Approx. 0.8km · 30 min · Elevation ↑250m steep incline
Difficulty

From Huibangsa Temple to Kkaldakgogae Pass is the steepest section of this course. As its name suggests, derived from the gasping sound 'kkaldak-kkaldak,' a steep incline continues. The wooden deck stairs are well-maintained, but this section is very physically demanding.

This section is the main challenge of the beginner course. Take plenty of breaks and ascend slowly. It can be especially slippery during descent.
3
Kkaldakgogae Pass → Yeonhwabong Peak Summit
Approx. 1.9km · 50 min · Gentle ridge
Difficulty

Beyond Kkaldakgogae Pass, the gentle and wide ridge characteristic of Sobaeksan National Park unfolds. From the summit of Yeonhwabong Peak (1,394m), enjoy panoramic views of Taebaeksan National Park, Woraksan National Park, and Songnisan National Park on a clear day. Sobaeksan National Park Observatory is located right next to it.

In spring (late May to early June), the Yeonhwabong Ridge transforms into a pink azalea tunnel. The Yeonhwabong Peak observatory is a famous spot for azalea photos.
4
Yeonhwabong Peak → Descend to starting point
Approx. 3.7km · 1 hr 20 min · Descent
Difficulty

Descend the same path you ascended, towards Kkaldakgogae Pass → Huibangsa Temple → Huibangpokpo Waterfall → Huibang Information Center. During the descent, you can rest briefly at Huibangpokpo Waterfall and enjoy the sound of the water.

Juryeong Course Link Option: After an additional 3km from Yeonhwabong Peak to Je2-Yeonhwabong Peak, it is possible to descend to Juryeong (requires 2 vehicles). Recommended for those with ample time.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots or athletic shoes (waterproofing recommended)
  • Windproof jacket
  • Trekking poles (for descending Kkaldakgogae Pass)
  • Winter: crampons essential
🌸

Azalea Season

  • Azalea peak from late May to early June
  • Yeonhwabong Peak ~ Je1-Yeonhwabong Peak Azalea Tunnel
  • Birobong Ridge pink carpet
  • Most crowded during azalea season → Early start recommended
❄

Winter snow blossoms and hoarfrost

  • Yeonhwabong hoarfrost peak in January-February
  • Spectacular icefall at Huibangpokpo Falls (Dec-Feb)
  • Kkaldakgogae Pass crampon wearing section
  • Caution for strong winds on the ridge · full winter gear required

Transportation Information

  • Yeongju Station → Huibangsa Temple bus service
  • Huibang Information Center Parking (Paid)
  • Danyang IC → Huibangsa Temple 30 min
  • Sobaeksan National Park 054-638-6196
🌟

Sobaeksan National Park Observatory

  • Near Yeonhwabong Peak Summit (5-minute walk)
  • Operated by Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute
  • Night Star Observation Program (Reservation)
  • Connects to Seolyeonhwa Astronomical Observatory Tour Course
📱

Useful Information

  • Entry hours: Summer season 04:00~14:00
  • Sobaeksan National Park 054-638-6196
  • Blackyak 100 Famous Mountains (Birobong Peak)
  • Expedition possible in conjunction with the Juryeong Course

📊 Elevation Profile — Eouigok Ranger Station → Birobong Peak Round Trip

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Eouigok Ranger Station→ Eouigoksamgeori→ Yew tree colony→ Birobong Peak 1,439m ★→ (Descent in reverse direction)→ 🅿 Eouigok Return

Course Features

  • ✅ Shortest course to Sobaeksan National Park summit (Birobong Peak) — 5.1km one-way, approx. 2 hours 40 minutes
  • ✅ Start on the refreshing forest trail of Eouigok Valley — best for summer vacation hiking
  • ✅ Yew tree habitat just before Birobong Peak — Spectacular cluster of natural monument yew trees
  • ✅ 360-degree view from Birobong Peak summit — Yeonhwabong and Gukmangbong ridges at a glance
  • After Eouigok Valley, the ridge section is a continuous uphill climb.
  • Strong winds at the summit — windproof jacket essential
Detailed Route by Section
Exit
Depart from Eouigok Information Center
405m above sea level · Gagok-myeon, Danyang-gun, Chungbuk

Depart from Eouigok Information Center (Saebat Amusement Park Parking Lot, 405m above sea level). After checking the trail at the information center, begin your ascent. Restrooms are available at the information center.

Eouigok is the lowest trailhead (405m) in Sobaeksan National Park, but the altitude difference to Birobong Peak (1,439m) is 1,034m, requiring significant physical exertion. Depart after thorough preparation.
1
Eouigok Information Center → Eouigok Samgeori
Approx. 3.0km · 1 hr 10 min · Elevation ↑520m
Difficulty

This section follows Eouigok Valley. Walk along the forest path, listening to the clear stream, until you reach Eouigoksamgeori (Birobong Peak and Gukmangbong Peak junction). The valley is cool during the summer vacation season.

At Eouigoksamgeori, the path splits towards Gukmangbong Peak and Birobong Peak. Birobong Peak is straight ahead (left). Checking the signpost is essential.
2
Eouigoksamgeori → Yew Tree Community → Birobong Peak
Approx. 2.1km · 1 hr 30 min · Elevation ↑514m
Difficulty

From Eouigoksamgeori, the uphill climb continues along the ridge to Birobong Peak. Just before Birobong Peak, a community of yew trees (Natural Monument) unfolds. Yew trees live for over a thousand years, and the scenery, especially with winter snow blossoms, is overwhelming.

Birobong Peak (1,439m) is the highest peak of Sobaeksan National Park. On a clear day, enjoy 360-degree views of Yeonhwabong Peak, Gukmangbong Peak, Taebaeksan National Park, and Woraksan National Park. A photo at the summit marker is a must!
3
Birobong Peak → Eouigok original point descent
Approx. 5.1km · 1 hr 40 min · Descent
Difficulty

Descend the same path you ascended, towards Eouigoksamgeori → Eouigok Information Center. Alternatively, you can descend towards Cheondongsamgeori (towards Cheondong parking lot, vehicle required).

During descent, be mindful of your knees on the steep section going down from the ridge towards the valley. Actively use trekking poles.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots (waterproof, ankle support)
  • Trekking poles
  • Windproof and waterproof jacket
  • Winter: crampons + winter clothing
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • More than 1.5L of water (elevation difference 1,034m)
  • Energy bar, snacks
  • Eouigok Valley: Drinking water available (purification recommended)
  • Danyang Garlic Gukbap · Natural Trout Sashimi
⏰

Entry restriction hours

  • Summer season: Entry permitted from 04:00 to 14:00
  • Winter season entry available from 05:00 to 13:00
  • Entry restricted after 13:00-14:00
  • Access restricted during strong winds at the summit

Yew tree colony

  • Natural Monument area just before Birobong Peak
  • Yew tree: Can live for over 1,000 years
  • Winter snow blossoms + yew trees = best snow scenery
  • Do not stray from the trail (to prevent damage)
🚗

Transportation Information

  • Access Danyang Station/Danyang Bus Terminal
  • Eouigok Parking Lot = Saebat Amusement Park Parking Lot
  • Juryeong~Eouigok Shuttle 'Sobaek Nae Cha Butakhae'
  • Sobaeksan National Park 043-422-5631
🔄

Modified Course

  • Eouigok→Birobong Peak→Cheondongsamgeori→Cheondong
  • Round trip 14.4km when connected with Cheondong Course
  • One-way possible with 2 vehicles
  • Additional connection to Gukmangbong Peak (Eouigok starting point)

📊 Elevation Profile — Juknyeong → Birobong Peak → Gukmangbong Peak → Eouigok Traverse

Overall Course Flow
🅿 Juknyeong Rest Area 700m→ Je2-yeonhwabong Peak 1,357m→ Yeonhwabong Peak 1,394m→ Je1-yeonhwabong Peak 1,386m→ Cheondongsamgeori→ Birobong Peak 1,439m ★→ Gukmangbong 1,421m→ Sangwolbong Peak→ Neujeunmaegijae Pass→ 🏁 Eouigok Parking Lot

Course Features

  • 🏆 Sobaeksan National Park's Representative Ridge Traverse Course — Juknyeong (700m) ~ Eouigok (405m) 21.1km
  • ✅ Connects 5 peaks: Je2-Yeonhwabong, Yeonhwabong, Je1-Yeonhwabong, Birobong, Gukmangbong
  • ✅ Pink carpet along the entire course during the spring azalea season (late May to early June) — Korea's best azalea spot
  • ✅ Mostly gentle ridges — despite being 22km, the elevation difference is mild, making a one-day traverse possible
  • Departure from Juknyeong between 6-7 AM is essential (arrive before 14:00 due to Eouigok entry restrictions).
  • Requires 2 vehicles at Juryeong/Eouigok or use of a car delivery service
Detailed Route by Section
Exit
Depart from Juryeong Trailhead Information Center
700m above sea level · Daegang-myeon, Danyang-gun, Chungbuk · 6-7 AM

Depart from Juryeong Trailhead Information Center (near Juryeong Rest Area parking lot, 700m above sea level). Leave your car at Eouigok or Cheondong, or use a car delivery service. Must depart between 6-7 AM (entry restricted at 2 PM).

Vehicle delivery service available via the 'Sobaek Nae Chareul Butakae' Kakao Channel. Juknyeong ↔ Eouigok approx. 47,000 won. Reservations required at least 1-2 days in advance.
1
Juryeong → Je2-Yeonhwabong Peak → Yeonhwabong Peak
Approx. 7.1km · 2 hrs · Gentle forest road ridge
Difficulty

From Juryeong to Je2-Yeonhwabong Peak (1,357m), the section is relatively gentle due to a forest road. Enjoy panoramic views from the Je2-Yeonhwabong Peak Observatory. Pass the Sobaeksan Observatory to reach Yeonhwabong Peak (1,394m). It's a famous spot for spring azaleas and winter hoarfrost.

The Juryeong~Je2-Yeonhwabong Peak section (4.2km) is gentle, including a forest road. The Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute's Sobaeksan Observatory is located directly below Yeonhwabong Peak.
2
Yeonhwabong Peak → Je1-Yeonhwabong Peak → Cheondong Samgeori
Approx. 2.8km · 1 hr · Azalea tunnel ridge
Difficulty

The approximately 300m section from Yeonhwabong Peak to Je1-Yeonhwabong Peak (1,386m) is a famous azalea tunnel. In late May, during the peak azalea season, experience a fantastic walk between azaleas on both sides. It is possible to descend from Cheondong Samgeori to Cheondong Parking Lot (vehicle needed).

Je1-yeonhwabong Peak is a Blackyak BAC certification point. The ridge scenery where azaleas and the sky meet in spring is called the 'Alps of Korea'.
3
Cheondongsamgeori → Birobong Peak
Approx. 1.4km · 40 min · Yew tree cluster section
Difficulty

From Cheondongsamgeori, pass through the yew tree community to Birobong Peak. After a sufficient meal and rest at the summit of Birobong Peak (1,439m), proceed towards Gukmangbong Peak. Beware of strong winds at the summit.

Take a short break at the Juniper Observation Post on Birobong Peak summit. This is the halfway point of the traverse. Check your physical condition, weather, and time, then decide whether to proceed with the second half.
4
Birobong Peak → Gukmangbong Peak → Sangwolbong Peak → Neujeunmaegijae Pass
Approx. 5.5km · 2 hrs · Key ridge traverse
Difficulty

From Birobong Peak, you will traverse a windy ridge to Gukmangbong Peak (1,421m). Gukmangbong Peak is famous for its strange rock formations and azalea clusters. After passing Sangwolbong Peak, you will arrive at Neujeunmaegijae Pass (the descent point for Eouigok).

Gukmangbong Peak (legend says King Gongmin of Goryeo looked out from here, longing for the capital) is quieter than Birobong Peak and has more vibrant azaleas. Be sure to visit.
5
Neujeunmaegijae Pass → Eouigok Parking Lot descent
Approx. 4.3km · 1 hr 30 min · Valley descent
Difficulty

Descend from Neujeunmaegijae towards Eouigok. After the initial 300m steep slope, it's a gentle valley path. In winter, the frozen ice and icefalls of Eouigok Valley are a spectacular sight. The traverse concludes at Eouigok Parking Lot (Saebat Amusement Park).

The initial 300m steep section of Neujeunmaegijae requires crampons (in winter) or trekking poles. Approximately 4-5 km until descent completion — speed adjustment needed.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots (waterproof, ankle support)
  • 2 trekking poles
  • Windproof and waterproof jacket
  • Winter: crampons, winter clothing, hot packs
  • Headlamp (early start)
🥗

Food & Hydration

  • Carry more than 2L of water
  • Energy bar, packed lunch (2 meals)
  • Energy replenishment is essential at Birobong Peak summit
  • Danyang Garlic Pork Ribs · Olgaengi-guk (Freshwater Snail Soup)
🚗

Car shuttle service

  • Kakao 'Sobaek My Car, Please'
  • Juryeong↔Eouigok approximately 47,000 won
  • Cheondong ↔ Eouigok approx. 23,000~30,000 KRW
  • Advance reservation required 1-2 days prior
🌸

Azalea Season Traverse

  • Azalea peak from late May to early June
  • Yeonhwabong Azalea Tunnel 300m
  • Birobong pink ridge carpet
  • Gukmangbong Peak Purple and Hot Pink Azalea Colony
⏰

Time Planning

  • Must depart Juryeong between 6-7 AM
  • Target Birobong Peak 11 AM - 12 PM
  • Check Eouigok entry control before 14:00
  • If late, shorten by descending Cheondong
📡

Safety & Navigation

  • Save course in GPS app beforehand
  • Ridge strong winds, felt temperature below -15°C
  • Cheondongsamgeori·Neujeunmaegijae Escape Route
  • Sobaeksan National Park 043-422-5631
