Yangmingshan National Park Course 3 Hiking Playbook

This is an interactive guide divided into Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced levels for day-trip courses to Yangmingshan National Park and Qixingshan that are good to visit from Taipei. It is structured based on the most frequently walked representative routes, centered around Xiaoyoukeng, Lengshuikeng, and Qingtiangang.

Course Flow

Xiaoyoukeng→Qixingshan Main Peak→Out and Back

Elevation Profile

The shortest and most representative course, which goes from Xiaoyoukeng to the summit of Qixingshan and back, is often guided as approximately 1.6km to the summit and 3.2km round trip, with actual reviews frequently showing times around 1 hour 40 minutes. This is the least strenuous introductory course for day-trippers in Taipei.

Sectional Route Guide

Departure
Depart from Xiaoyoukeng
0km · Sulfur Fumarole Observation Deck
⌄

Pass the bus stop and observatory, then enter the stairs towards Qixingshan. Since there are many stairs from the beginning, it's best to find your rhythm slowly.

Before starting, it's recommended to first see the Xiaoyoukeng fumaroles and sulfur terrain to fully experience the unique characteristics of Yangmingshan National Park.
1
Xiaoyoukeng → Qixingshan Main Peak
1.6km · 50~60 min
⌄

The main sections consist of stone and wooden stairs, and on clear days, views towards downtown Taipei are also open. Although the elevation is not high, the continuous stairs can be quite breathless.

If the wind is strong, the perceived temperature at the summit will be lower, so it's a good idea to prepare a thin windbreaker.
Lower
Descend to the starting point after enjoying the summit
1.6km · 40~50 min
⌄

Descend the same way you came up. After rain, stairs and rocks can be slippery, so shorten your stride when descending.

After descending, you can immediately return to the city by bus, making it suitable for a half-day trip.

Preparation & Gear

  • Trekking shoes with good grip are recommended over sneakers.
  • Water 500mL-1L
  • Windbreaker x1

Points

  • Xiaoyoukeng Sulfur Fumaroles
  • Summit View
  • Short and intense hike near Taipei

Transportation

  • Access by bus from Taipei city center
  • Many hikers on weekends
  • Morning departure is advantageous for securing views.

Representative Route

Course Flow

Xiaoyoukeng→Qixingshan Main Peak→Qixingshan East Peak→Lengshuikeng

Elevation Profile

The most common Qixingshan traverse route leading to Lengshuikeng is introduced as approximately 4km, taking an average of 2.5-3.5 hours. The most balanced configuration is to ascend from Xiaoyoukeng, connect the Main Peak and East Peak, and descend to the Lengshuikeng hot spring area.

Sectional Route Guide

Departure
Xiaoyoukeng → Main Peak
1.6km · 50~60 min
⌄

You ascend via the same path as the beginner course, but the descent route changes after the summit. The long stair section makes it advisable not to overexert yourself from the start.

Even when fog reduces visibility, the signposts are relatively well-maintained.
1
Jubong Peak → Dongbong Peak
0.5km · 15~20 min
⌄

Follow the ridge to Dongbong. The ascents and descents are short, but it can be slippery depending on the wind and humidity.

The ridge view from Dongbong Peak is often more open and refreshing than from Jubong Peak.
Lower
Dongbong → Naengsugang
1.5~1.9km · Around 1 hour
⌄

The Lengshuikeng route mixes stairs and dirt paths, and after descending, it's convenient to use the hot spring area and bus stop. The strain on the knees is slightly greater than a round trip to Xiaoyoukeng.

After descending, it's great to enjoy the cold water foot bath and hot spring atmosphere together.

Gear

  • Trekking shoes recommended
  • Water 1L
  • Light outer layer for fog

Highlights

  • Connect 2 Peaks
  • Lengshuikeng Hot Spring Atmosphere
  • Convenient public transport connections

Caution

  • Stairs are slippery on rainy days
  • Strong winds at the summit
  • Need to check descent bus schedule

Course Flow

Xiaoyoukeng→Jubong Peak→Dongbong→Menghuanhu Lake→Lengshuikeng→Qingtiangang

Elevation Profile

The route from Xiaoyoukeng, passing through Qixingshan Main Peak and East Peak, then connecting to Menghuanhu, Lengshuikeng, and Qingtiangang, is widely recommended at approximately 5.5km and takes around 3 to 3 hours 30 minutes. This route strongly resembles a full Yangmingshan course, as it combines a summit hike with grassland scenery and hot spring areas.

Sectional Route Guide

Departure
Xiaoyoukeng → Qixingshan Main Peak
1.6km · 50~60 min
⌄

The core ascent to the summit is the same. On crowded days, the initial stairs may slow you down.

Departing early in the morning is good for reducing both fog and crowds.
1
Jubong Peak → Dongbong Peak → Monghwanho Lake
1.2km · 35~45 min
⌄

Past the ridge, the trail continues towards Menghuan Pond. If the weather is clear, it's great to enjoy both the grassland and crater-like terrain.

The atmosphere of Menghuanhu Lake changes significantly depending on the season and water level.
2
Menghuanhu Lake → Lengshuikeng
1.0km · 25~35 min
⌄

Descending towards Lengshuikeng, you can experience the geothermal area and hot spring atmosphere. After the descent, there's a resting point, making it feel more relaxed.

It's good to take a short break at Lengshuikeng and then continue to Qingtiangang.
Lower
Lengshuikeng → Qingtiangang
1.7km · 40~50 min
⌄

This is the final section that connects to meadow terrain. Unlike Qixingshan, which is mainly forest and ridges, this area features expansive pasture scenery.

On rainy or foggy days, visibility in the grassland section can decrease rapidly, so consider bus schedules and sunset times.

Gear

  • Trekking shoes and windbreaker
  • Water 1-1.5L
  • Snacks and power bank

Attractions

  • Volcanic Terrain + Summit
  • Menghuanhu Wetland
  • Qingtiangang Grassland

Operating Tips

  • Different termini, check return bus routes
  • Bus congestion possible on weekends
  • Allow extra time during fog season

Safe Return Timeline and Last Bus Guide by Course

Last Bus Reverse Calculation Guide for Descent by Course

Beginner Course

Beginner: Xiaoyoukeng Out and Back

  • Trailhead Exit Point:Xiaoyoukeng Bus Stop
  • Recommended Return Route:Xiaoyoukeng ➔ 108 Shuttle Bus ➔ Yangmingshan Terminal ➔ Red 5 or 260 Bus ➔ Downtown
  • Descent completion deadline: Before 16:30
  • ※ The 108 shuttle bus departs the terminal at 17:30 to complete its circuit. Therefore, to avoid missing the last bus, it is recommended to safely board at the mountain stop, Xiaoyoukeng, around 16:30.
Intermediate Course

Intermediate: Xiaoyoukeng ➔ Lengshuikeng Traverse

  • Trailhead Exit Point:Lengshuikeng Bus Stop
  • Recommended Return Route:Lengshuikeng ➔ Small 15 bus (direct to Jiantan Station) or 108 shuttle ➔ Terminal transfer
  • Descent completion deadline:
    Weekdays: Before 16:00 / Weekends: Before 17:00
  • ※ From Lengshuikeng, you can go directly to Jiantan Station via the Small 15 microbus. However, if the bus departing from Qingtiangang is full, there is a high possibility it will pass Lengshuikeng without stopping. If it is full, you must take bus 108 to the terminal and transfer.
Advanced Course

Advanced: Xiaoyoukeng ➔ Qingtiangang Traverse

  • Trailhead Exit Point:Qingtiangang Bus Stop (Terminal)
  • Recommended Return Route:Qingtiangang ➔ Small 15 bus (board at the terminal, can sit) or 108 shuttle ➔ Transfer at the terminal
  • Descent completion deadline:
    Weekdays: Before 16:00 / Weekends: Before 17:00
  • ※ The last Small 15 bus from Qingtiangang departs punctually at 16:20 on weekdays and 17:30 on weekends. If the waiting line is long, you will have to take the next bus, so it is strongly recommended to wait at the bus stop at least 30 minutes before the last bus.
Useful Tips and Precautions
Essential Public Transportation Tips
  • Check EasyCard balance:There are no card charging stations in the mountainous areas of Yangmingshan National Park. Please check and charge your card with a sufficient balance of at least NT$ 200 at an MRT station in the city beforehand.
  • Two-way tag when boarding/alighting:For Taipei City buses, you must tap your card both when boarding and alighting for the fare to be correctly charged and for transfer discounts to apply.
  • Weekend Crowds:On pleasant weather weekends between 2 PM and 5 PM, there can be a large crowd of hikers waiting for the downhill bus, potentially leading to queues of 30 minutes or more. If possible, it is more comfortable to start your hike before 8 AM and descend before 2-3 PM.
  • Weather Deterioration Fallback:The summit of Yangmingshan National Park experiences rapid temperature changes and often has dense fog or strong winds. Buses may operate on a reduced schedule or be suspended due to heavy rain or typhoon warnings, so please reconfirm real-time bus information on the official website before departure.
