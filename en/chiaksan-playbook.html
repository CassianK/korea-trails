📊 Elevation Profile — Guryongsa~Seryeom Falls Course (Guryongsa Parking Lot~Seryeom Falls Roundtrip)

Overall Course Flow
Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot→Guryongsa Temple→Daegok Safety Center→Seryeom Falls→Return to Daegok Safety Center→Return to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot

Course Features

  • From Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot (280m), past Daegok Safety Center (350m) to Seryeom Falls (450m), a gentle and flat path with few stairs continues along the Hwangjangmok forest trail and valley path.
  • ⚠️ Caution: Falling rocks in some sections
  • ⚠️ Caution: Icy conditions on trails adjacent to valleys in winter
Detailed Route by Section
Dep.
Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot → Guryongsa Temple
Approx. 1.0km · 25 min · Uphill

Depart from Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot and head towards Guryongsa Temple (300m above sea level). A thousand-year-old temple. Please proceed with caution for safety.

The slope may change in the section near Guryongsa Temple, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
2
Guryongsa Temple → Daegok Safety Center
Approx. 0.9km · 22 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Guryongsa Temple and head towards Daegok Safety Center (350m above sea level). Restrooms and rest area. Please proceed with caution for safety.

The slope near the Daegok Safety Center can change, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
3
Daegok Safety Center → Seryeom Falls
Approx. 1.1km · 27 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Daegok Safety Center and head towards Seryeom Falls (450m above sea level). This is the turnaround point, a 6.7m high, two-tiered waterfall. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Seryeompokpo Waterfall may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
4
Seryeom Falls → Daegok Safety Center Return
Approx. 1.1km · 16 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Seryeompokpo Waterfall and head back to Daegok Safety Center (350m above sea level). Via the descent path. Please proceed with caution for your safety.

The slope near the Daegok Safety Center return point can change, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
5
Return to Daegok Safety Center → Return to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot
Approx. 1.9km · 28 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Daegok Safety Center return point and head towards Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot return point (280m above sea level). This is the end point. Please proceed with caution.

The slope may change in the section near the return to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots or trekking shoes
  • Plenty of drinking water (1L or more)
  • Seasonal windbreaker/winter clothing
  • Simple snacks (chocolate, nuts)

Public Transportation Access

  • Guryongsa Temple Area: Take city bus No. 41 from KTX Wonju Station or Wonju Intercity Bus Terminal and get off at Guryongsa Temple terminus (approx. 40-50 minutes, bus interval approx. 30-40 minutes).
  • Hwanggol District: Take city bus No. 82 from Wonju Station or downtown Wonju, get off at Hwanggol bus stop, and walk approximately 1.5km to Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center (bus frequency is low, 7-10 times a day, so taxi use is recommended).
🌸

Recommended Season & Attractions

  • Autumn foliage of Guryonggyegok Valley and eulalia grass on the main ridge of Birobong Peak
  • Silver snowscape and rime ice around the Birobong Peak stone pagoda in winter
🛡

Course Safety Rules

  • Caution: Falling rocks in some sections
  • Caution: Icy conditions on trails adjacent to valleys in winter
  • During winter, extreme caution is advised for icy, steep slopes on the Sadaribyeongchang-gil trail and near Birobong Peak due to freezing and risk of falls. Access is restricted during weather advisories, and there is a risk of valley flooding during heavy summer rains.
🍱

Nearby Amenities

  • Main Trailheads: Guryong Visitor Center (262 Guryongsa-ro, Socho-myeon, Wonju-si, Gangwon Special Self-Governing Province) or Hwanggol Visitor Center (360 Hwanggol-gil, Socho-myeon, Wonju-si, Gangwon Special Self-Governing Province)
  • Chiaksan is so challenging that there's a joke, 'It makes your teeth chatter and your spirit rise,' especially the steep stair sections of the Sadaribyeongchang Course. We strongly recommend wearing knee pads, hiking poles, and hiking boots with good traction.
  • The three large stone pagodas (Sansintap, Chilseongtap, Yongwangtap) on Birobong Peak are a historic folk cultural heritage, meticulously built alone over approximately 10 years starting in 1962 by the late Mr. Yong Jin-su, a resident of Wonju, who received a divine revelation in a dream.
📱

National Park Information

  • Chiaksan National Park Office: 062-227-1187
  • Summit Certification: Blackyak 100 Famous Mountains (Birobong)
  • Reservation System: reservation.knps.or.kr

📊 Elevation Profile — Hwanggol Course (Hwanggol Trailhead Support Center~Ipsoksa Temple~Birobong Peak Roundtrip)

Overall Course Flow
Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center→Ipdaeseoksa Temple→Hwanggolsamgeori Junction→Jwineomijae Observatory→Birobong Peak→Return to Jwineomijae Observatory→Return to Hwanggolsamgeori Junction→Return to Ipdaeseoksa Temple→Return to Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center

Course Features

  • From Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center (400m) to IpSeoksa Temple (720m) is a steep cement forest road. From IpSeoksa Temple to Hwanggolsamgeori Junction (1040m) is a steep scree slope and stone stair 'Kkaltakgogae' (a very steep pass). After that, it is a gentle main ridge to Birobong Peak (1288m).
  • ⚠️ Caution: Steep, strenuous climb from Ipsoksa Temple to Hwanggol Samgeori, significant energy expenditure
  • ⚠️ Caution: Risk of knee injury when descending the Ipsoksa Temple section
Detailed Route by Section
Dep.
Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center → IpSeoksa Temple
Approx. 1.6km · 40 min · Uphill

Depart from Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center and head to IpSeoksa Temple (elevation 720m). This is an ancient temple from the Silla Dynasty with a view of IpSeokdae Rock. Please proceed with caution.

The section near Ipoksan Temple may have changing slopes, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
2
Ipdaeseoksa Temple → Hwanggol Samgeori
Approx. 1.2km · 30 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Ipoksan Temple and head towards Hwanggol Samgeori (1040m above sea level). This is where the main ridge merges and the steep climb ends. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Hwanggolsamgeori Junction may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
3
Hwanggolsamgeori Junction → Jwineomijae Observatory
Approx. 0.5km · 7 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Hwanggolsamgeori Junction and head to Jwineomijae Observatory (elevation 1000m). This is a viewpoint for Wonju city. Please proceed with caution.

The section near Jwineomijae Observatory may have changing slopes, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
4
Jwineomijae Observatory → Birobong
Approx. 0.8km · 20 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Jwineomijae Observatory and head towards Birobong (1288m above sea level). This is the highest peak of Chiaksan National Park, with 3 stone pagodas. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Birobong Peak can change, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
5
Birobong Peak → Jwineomijae Observatory Return
Approx. 0.8km · 12 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Birobong Peak and head towards Jwineomijae Observatory return point (1000m above sea level). This is a ridge on the descent path. Please proceed with caution.

The section near the return to Jwineomijae Observatory may have changing slopes, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
6
Return to Jwineomijae Observatory → Return to Hwanggol Samgeori
Approx. 0.5km · 12 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from the return to Jwineomijae Observatory and head towards the return to Hwanggol Samgeori (1040m above sea level). This is the descent fork. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near the return to Hwanggolsamgeori Junction may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
7
Return to Hwanggolsamgeori Junction → Return to IpSeoksa Temple
Approx. 1.2km · 18 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from the return to Hwanggolsamgeori Junction and head back to IpSeoksa Temple (elevation 720m). Be cautious of the steep descent. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Ipdaeseoksa Temple may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
8
Return to Ipdaeseoksa Temple → Return to Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center
Approx. 1.6km · 24 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Ipdaeseoksa Temple and head back to Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center (400m above sea level). Round trip completed. Please proceed with caution for your safety.

The slope near the return to Hwanggol Trailhead Information Center may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
Tips & Precautions

Essential Gear

  • Hiking boots or trekking shoes
  • Plenty of drinking water (1L or more)
  • Seasonal windbreaker/winter clothing
  • Simple snacks (chocolate, nuts)

📊 Elevation Profile — Sadaribyeongchang Course (Guryongsa~Seryeom Falls~Sadaribyeongchang~Birobong Peak Roundtrip)

Overall Course Flow
Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot→Guryongsa Temple→Seryeom Falls→Sadaribyeongchang→Birobong Peak→Chilseokpokpo Waterfall→Seryeom Falls Return→Return to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot

Course Features

  • From Seryeompokpo Waterfall (450m) to Birobong Peak (1288m) via Sadaribyeongchang (900m), a steep rocky ridge and continuous iron stairs require strong physical stamina. The descent returns via a relatively gentle valley path (passing Chilseokpokpo Waterfall).
  • ⚠️ Caution: Extremely steep slopes and rocky cliffs on Sadaribyeongchang-gil, risk of falling
  • ⚠️ Caution: Extreme winds and frequent icy conditions near Birobong Peak in winter, risk of falling
Detailed Route by Section
Dep.
Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot → Guryongsa Temple
Approx. 1.0km · 25 min · Uphill

Depart from Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot and head towards Guryongsa Temple (300m above sea level). A thousand-year-old temple. Please proceed with caution for safety.

The slope may change in the section near Guryongsa Temple, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
2
Guryongsa Temple → Seryeom Falls
Approx. 2.0km · 50 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Guryongsa Temple and head towards Seryeom Falls (450m above sea level). This is the starting point of the main hiking trail, the Sadaribyeongchang/Valley Trail fork. Please proceed with caution for safety.

The slope near Seryeompokpo Waterfall may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
3
Seryeom Falls → Sadaribyeongchang
Approx. 1.4km · 35 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Seryeompokpo Waterfall and head towards Sadaribyeongchang (900m above sea level). Steep rocky ridge and stair area. Please proceed with caution for your safety.

The slope near Sadaribyeongchang can change, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
4
Sadaribyeongchang → Birobong Peak
Approx. 1.3km · 32 min · Uphill
Difficulty

Depart from Sadaribyeongchang and head towards Birobong Peak (1288m above sea level). This is the summit of Chiaksan National Park, with 3 stone pagodas. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Birobong Peak can change, so don't overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
5
Birobong Peak → Chilseok Falls
Approx. 1.5km · 22 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Birobong Peak and head towards Chilseok Falls (700m above sea level). This is a rest area on the valley trail descent route. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Chilseokpokpo Waterfall may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
6
Chilseokpokpo Waterfall → Return to Seryeom Waterfall
Approx. 1.3km · 19 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Chilseokpokpo Waterfall and head back to Seryeom Waterfall (elevation 450m). This is the junction of the valley trail and Sadaribyeongchang. Please proceed with caution.

The slope near Seryeompokpo Waterfall may change, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
7
Return to Seryeompokpo Waterfall → Return to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot
Approx. 3.0km · 45 min · Downhill
Difficulty

Depart from Seryeompokpo Waterfall and head back to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot (280m above sea level). Round trip completed. Please proceed with caution for your safety.

The slope may change in the section near the return to Guryongsa Temple Parking Lot, so do not overexert yourself and maintain your own pace.
